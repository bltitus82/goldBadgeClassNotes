Introducing Louie: the man, the myth, the legend.

I was born in a small town, the oldest child, to two people I'd never met before, who had no experience being parents.

The situation was ripe for comedy. I was learning to be human, and they were learning to be parents. What's a boy to do? In my case, I was a good baby. The best baby, to be honest. You've never met such a beautiful, agreeable kid. I had a brother and he was a monster. He made me look even better by comparison.

Things went well until my teenage years, when things went wrong.

What you need to understand is that I wanted to be a good kid. But I would have these wonderful ideas that would just pop into my mind and before I knew it, I was joyriding around camp with a dozen of my friends all piled on to the counselor's golf cart, him chasing us with the tails of his polo shirt untucked and trying to keep his pants from falling down under his overlarge stomach. He probably would have caught us anyway, but we crashed it into the volleyball pit and got stuck in the sand. True story.
